Atletico MG and Bahia ultimately settled for a share of the spoils in a hard-fought 1-1 draw, a result that keeps both sides locked in their respective mid-table pursuits.

Heading into this encounter, Atletico MG were eager to close the gap on the upper half of the standings and build momentum. However, their home form had been hampered by defensive stability issues, having failed to keep a clean sheet in 6 of their 8 home matches.

Bahia traveled for this fixture sitting just outside the top five, determined to sustain their positive momentum and push into the elite positions while relying on an away attacking efficiency that had seen them score in 7 of their 8 matches.

The first half unfolded with typical tactical caution until the stroke of half-time, when the deadlock was finally broken. Following a corner from the left, the ball was half-cleared by the defence but came back to the left for Bernard, who played a cross to the far post and Ruan's downward header went past the goalkeeper and into the opposite corner of the net (1-0).

Atletico MG's tendency to strike early was evident, as they had scored 56% of their goals in the first half this season. As was apparent before the game from their strong equalising rate, Bahia refused to back down after the break. In the 57th minute, from outside the box, Rodrigo Nestor played a low diagonal pass for Ademir on the right hand side of the box, and from a tight angle Ademir powers a rising shot inside the near corner (1-1).

Atletico MG shared the points, extending their unbeaten run to 2 matches in the league while recording their 5th consecutive home match without a clean sheet, leaving their defence at home ranked lowly.

The result keeps them searching for defensive consistency as they prepare for their next match at home against Bragantino.

Bahia extended their unbeaten run to 3 matches in the league, maintaining their high ranking in the away table and scoring in their 8th consecutive match. With goals in 18 of their 19 league matches this season, they will look to carry this offensive consistency into their upcoming home fixture against Corinthians.

  Domination Index calculation method ⌄

The Domination Index calculation is based on the following percentages:

   - Possession percentage
   - Percentage of match corners awarded to each team
   - Percentage of time each team has been in the lead

The calculation used is:
AVERAGE OF (% Possession x 4, % Corners and % Lead duration x 2)

'Domination' in football can be perceived in different ways. The goal of the Domination Index, as calculated here, is not about trying to determine things like 'who should have won' or which team has been simply attacking the most.

Sometimes a team has easily scored once or twice in the beginning of the game and then opts for a more reactive stance, with a focus on hindering the opponent's game. At the end of the match, the opponent might have had more possession, and even more shots, but that does not necessarily mean they have dominated the other team.

Considering possession as only one of the factors, which also include corners (clearly indicating a form of pressure on the opponent's defence), and how much of the 90-minute match duration each team has been leading, allows to also account for when teams have simply been busy controling the game, without needing to attack a lot.
